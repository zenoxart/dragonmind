Philosophical Movements & Schools

  • Heroism
    The philosophy of the hero, focusing on courage, virtue, and the pursuit of greatness. Heroism explores the role of the individual who rises above adversity for the greater good.
  • Gnosticism
    An ancient spiritual tradition emphasizing personal knowledge (gnosis) of the divine, often contrasting the material and spiritual worlds and seeking enlightenment through inner revelation.
  • Stoicism
    A Hellenistic philosophy teaching the development of self-control and fortitude as a means to overcome destructive emotions, focusing on virtue, wisdom, and living in harmony with nature.
  • Hermetism
    A philosophical and esoteric tradition based on the writings attributed to Hermes Trismegistus, emphasizing the unity of all things, the correspondence between worlds, and the pursuit of spiritual transformation.
  • Monadism
    The metaphysical view that reality is composed of simple, indivisible, and eternal substances called monads, as described by Leibniz, each reflecting the universe in its own unique way.
